Department of Dentistry

Providing dental care, intensive care, and pain management with compassion and innovation.

History

Historical Background The department of Dentistry made a small beginning in1956 when the 3rd Druk Gyalpo commanded the construction of the first allopathic hospital in Langjophaka, Thimphu. When the hospital was moved to its present location in 1974 as a general hospital, the dental unit moved along with it. Like in all other fields, the department gradually expanded its services over the years with the addition of more dentists. When an additional OPD block was added in 2002, the department increased its working space of six chambers including a prosthodontic laboratory and a VIP clinic. This was further boosted in 2009 after completion of the new hospital complex when one wing the ground floor was delegated to Dental department. The department is the largest dental centre in the country providing primary, secondary and tertiary services and also serves as the teaching department of JDWNRH for the Faculty of Nursing and Public Health (FoNPH) under the Khesar Gyalpo University of Medical Sciences of Bhutan (KGUMSB).

Human Resources Department of Dentistry currently has five specialists and three General Duty Dental Surgeons assisted by 18 Dental Hygienists and 17 Dental Technicians. Three dentists are on temporary attachment to update skills and knowledge after graduating from colleges. When two staff return from postgraduate studies, an orthodontist and a Dental Physician will add up to the existing pool for more advance services before mid of 2018
